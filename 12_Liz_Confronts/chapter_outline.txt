CHAPTER 12: LIZ CONFRONTS
==========================

CHAPTER PITCH:
Liz stands at her sister's kitchen table reading a news article on her phone—"Local Woman in Critical Condition After Illegal Memento Recording Gone Wrong"—and recognizes Simera's description despite no name being published, learning that her ex-girlfriend fell eighty-seven stories without proper safety equipment and survived only because she hit an awning twenty feet above ground, now lying comatose in a public ward with traumatic brain injury and every bone in her legs shattered, and as Liz rushes to the hospital and sees what remains of Simera—intubated, comatose, barely recognizable—she confronts Kael in the waiting room who admits with clinical detachment that Simera had become their most valuable creator but had started working illegal jobs when Heri couldn't meet her escalation demands, and in her grief and rage Liz realizes that the system killed Simera as surely as the fall did, that every person who bought those Mementos participated in her destruction, that Simera was always going to die because the market demanded it, and now she lies suspended between life and death, trapped in the very liminal space she'd been chasing all along.

FINAL NOTE:
This chapter reveals that Chapter 11 was real—she really fell, really nearly died. But the horror is she survived into a worse state: suspended between life and death, exactly the liminal space she'd been seeking. Liz returns but it's too late to save her, only to witness the destruction. The system's cruelty is exposed but continues. And the question raised: should Liz keep her on life support or let her finish dying? No good answer exists. Only choices between awful options. End with Liz at the bedside, facing an impossible decision, loving someone who's already gone.
