CHAPTER 13: THE AMNESIA DRUG
=============================

CHAPTER PITCH:
Simera wakes from her coma six weeks later into a body she doesn't recognize—paralyzed from the waist down, memories fractured and unreliable, unable to distinguish which experiences were hers and which were Mementos she consumed—and when Senna visits her hospital room with an offer she can barely comprehend through the fog of brain injury, explaining that black market Finale Mementos fetch higher prices if the creator doesn't know they're dying (the fear and confusion more authentic), offering her an amnesia drug that will erase the last year of her memories including all knowledge of Mementos, plus 200,000 units for her estate if she'll create one final fall, Simera looks at Liz sleeping in the chair beside her bed, understands dimly that this will destroy the woman she once loved, and says yes anyway because the only clear memory remaining is the certainty that she wants to stop existing and this is the only exit available to her broken body.

FINAL NOTE:
This is the chapter where Simera consciously, clearly chooses death. Not through addiction's fog, not through desperation's haze, but with what limited clarity her broken mind retains: she wants to stop existing and this is how. The amnesia drug adds a layer of horror—she'll die not knowing she chose it, experiencing it as terrified accident, which is exactly what makes it valuable. The system has commodified even her confusion. Liz can't stop her because Simera is choosing with full knowledge. And in two weeks, that knowledge will be gone, replaced by perfect authentic terror. End with the countdown begun, the choice made, the ending inevitable.
