# Chapter 11: The Breaking - V2

## Chapter Summary
**REVISED from "The Haunting"**: Liz is drowning in depression. Days after discovering what Simera did, she can't escape the guilt and pain. She sees herself as the burden destroying Simera. The physical pain continues, but emotional pain is worse—the knowledge that she's the reason Simera risked death. She can't eat, can't sleep, can't function. Simera tries everything to help, but nothing reaches Liz through the guilt. **One day, when the pain—physical and emotional—becomes unbearable, Liz makes a choice she swore she'd never make. She uses one of Simera's Mementos.** Just to escape for a little while. Just to feel something other than crushing guilt. She chooses something simple, calming. For the duration, pain stops. Guilt stops. She feels normal. When she comes back, reality crashes down, but she's discovered something terrifying: escape is possible. **She starts justifying it the same way Simera did.** The chapter ends with Liz rationalizing what she's done, and Simera discovering her—horror and understanding warring, recognizing the pattern.
