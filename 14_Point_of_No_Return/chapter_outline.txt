CHAPTER 14: POINT OF NO RETURN
===============================

CHAPTER PITCH:
During the two weeks before her scheduled fall, Simera experiences a strange liminal existence in the hospital—physical therapy for a body she knows she'll abandon, visits from Liz who speaks to her with desperate tenderness as if each word might be remembered, and moments of piercing clarity where she questions her decision but then the fog returns and with it the certainty that ending is correct—and on the final day, as Senna arrives with the amnesia drug in a small vial and the consent paperwork for Simera to sign before she forgets signing it, Liz makes one last attempt to stop her by asking "What if the you-who-forgets doesn't want to die? What if future-you is terrified and wants to live?" and Simera responds with the first fully articulate thing she's said since waking: "Then she'll feel what I've felt every day for years—trapped in a life she never chose, and I'm giving her the exit I never had," and she drinks the drug, and within hours her memories begin to dissolve like ice in summer until she wakes the next morning with no knowledge of Mementos or falling or choosing death, just a confused woman in a hospital bed asking a crying stranger "Who are you?" and Liz realizes she's now complicit in Simera's murder.
