CHAPTER 11: WHAT IS REAL
=========================

CHAPTER PITCH:
Simera wakes (or does she?) on the edge of a building she doesn't remember climbing, wind in her face, city below, no recording equipment attached, and as she tries to remember if this is a Memento she's consuming or reality she's inhabiting, if she's a creator being recorded or a consumer experiencing someone else's moment, or if she's simply standing here of her own volition about to jump without purpose or payment, she realizes with creeping horror that she genuinely cannot tell the difference anymore, and in this moment of existential vertigo she understands that she has achieved what she's been seeking all along—the complete dissolution of the boundary between lived experience and borrowed death—which means there is nothing left but to jump, so she does, and the chapter ends mid-fall without revealing whether this is real or Memento, whether she lives or dies, leaving the reader suspended in the same uncertainty that has consumed Simera's entire existence.

FINAL NOTE:
This is the horror chapter—not of monsters but of uncertainty. Simera can no longer distinguish real from Memento, life from death, self from other. The reader shouldn't be able to either. DO NOT RESOLVE THIS. Let it hang. Some readers will have theories (both valid). The ambiguity IS the point. She's falling—whether to death or wake-up, whether in reality or Memento, whether as creator or consumer, we genuinely cannot know. That's the ultimate dissolution. End without ending. Leave it suspended. Like Simera. Like everything. Forever falling, never landing. Or already landed. Or landing now. Who can tell?
