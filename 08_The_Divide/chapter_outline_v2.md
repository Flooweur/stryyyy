# Chapter 8: Small Victories - V2
## (Formerly "The Divide" - Significantly Revised)

## Chapter Summary
**MAJOR REVISION**: Instead of stopping creation and deciding never to do it again, this becomes a chapter of small victories and growth. Simera's content is doing well enough that they've made actual savings. They buy something nice for both of them—something small but meaningful that poverty had denied them. The financial breathing room gives Simera mental space to think beyond survival. **She starts looking into creating a recovering addict support group** (planting seeds for Chapter 15). Her nursing background and desire to help people resurfaces. **Her OCD is getting better** with reduced stress. We learn that **she really loves helping people**—this was always her core drive, even beneath the survival mode. Meanwhile, Liz sees these positive changes with hope mixed with wariness. Is this real stability or temporary? The role dynamics are subtly shifting: Simera working (creating), Liz home more with her pain.

**KEY CHANGE**: This is no longer about stopping—it's about a genuine period of improvement that makes the later descent more tragic. They're actually doing better.
