# Chapter 18: Lost in Fragments

## Chapter Summary
After the breaking point. Simera exists in fragments. Her reality is pieces that don't cohere. She can't hold onto continuous experience. Memories—her own and recorded—swirl without distinction. She's lost in the blur completely. Maybe she's still creating, or maybe she's just consuming, or maybe she can't tell the difference. Liz might be gone, or might be there but unreachable. The self as scattered fragments. The horror of consciousness without coherence. Brief moments of clarity that are more painful than confusion. The dissolution nearly complete.

## Atmosphere & Tone
Fragmentary narration, surreal, tragic, the pieces that don't fit together. Occasionally brutally clear. Mostly lost. The prose itself might fragment. Devastating.
