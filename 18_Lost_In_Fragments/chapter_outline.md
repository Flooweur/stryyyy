# Chapter 18: Dissolution

## Chapter Summary
The ending. Simera after Liz leaves, alone with her addiction. She exists in fragments now—the dissolution is nearly or fully complete. Reality and Mementos blur completely. She may still be creating, or consuming, or unable to tell the difference. Her memories are scattered pieces—Liz's face, moments from recordings, her own past, all swirling without distinction. Brief, painful moments of clarity where she surfaces and sees what she's become, what she's lost—these moments hurt more than the confusion. Mostly she's lost in the blur. The self as commodity has been sold piece by piece until nothing coherent remains. The prose itself might fragment to reflect her state. The ending should echo the opening fall—perhaps she does the falling Memento again, but now she doesn't know if it's real or recorded, if she's consuming or creating, if she's herself or someone else. The final image could be ambiguous: dissolution as ultimate freedom or ultimate horror. Has she found the escape she was seeking, or lost everything in seeking it? The ending should devastate—make the reader cry—while resonating with all the themes built throughout.
