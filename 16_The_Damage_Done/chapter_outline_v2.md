# Chapter 16: The Trying - V2

## Chapter Summary
**REVISED**: Simera starts working toward recovery—attending group meetings weekly, not using Mementos (or trying not to), attempting to rebuild normal life. It's hard. Incredibly hard. Her mind is damaged more than she realized. The fragmentation from months of use, the cravings, the counting (OCD worse from stress), the difficulty focusing on anything real. But she keeps trying. Meanwhile, Liz struggles more. She's trying—genuinely trying—but the pull is stronger for her. The cravings more intense. She attends meetings with Simera but seems distant. **One night, they try to connect physically—a sex scene that should be intimate but reveals how damaged they both are.** They go through motions. The physical response is there but hollow—even in orgasm, there's numbness, disconnection, emptiness. Both feel it: something fundamental is broken in both of them. Normal pleasure doesn't reach them anymore. They hold each other afterward in the silence of mutual damage. Days pass. Simera notices Liz slipping—subtle signs, familiar patterns. **Then: Simera comes home and discovers Liz in bed, device plugged in, experiencing a Memento. Not peaceful content—hardcore, intense, dangerous-looking. Liz relapsed. The promise broken. Everything falling apart.** The chapter ends with that discovery, Simera frozen in the doorway, watching Liz caught in a Memento, knowing what this means.
