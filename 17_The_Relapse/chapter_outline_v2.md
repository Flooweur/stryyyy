# Chapter 17: The Confrontation - V2

## Chapter Summary
**REVISED**: Picking up immediately from Chapter 16's discovery. Liz emerges from the Memento—disoriented, caught, defensive. The confrontation that follows is different from earlier ones. No mutual confession, no shared promises this time. Liz broke the promise they made together. Simera stayed clean (or tried to), Liz relapsed. The fight reveals how far gone Liz is: she's been using in secret for days, maybe weeks. Not just once—constantly, whenever Simera was at work or meetings. The content is increasingly dangerous, extreme, black market material. Simera is terrified—recognizes all the signs because she went through them herself, knows where this leads. "How long?" she asks. Liz admits: started again a few days after their first meeting. Never really stopped. Just hid it. The betrayal hurts but so does the recognition: Liz is worse off than Simera ever was, spiraling faster, deeper. Liz's mental state is fragmenting visibly. She can barely distinguish reality from Mementos. The confrontation doesn't resolve. They're in crisis—Liz's addiction exposed, both aware something must change but neither knowing how. The chapter ends unresolved: they just continue in crisis, no solution, both trapped.

## The Confrontation - Detailed Dialogue

**Opening**:
- Simera: "How long?"
- Liz: (trying to deflect) "How long what?"
- Simera: "How long have you been using again?"
- Liz: "I— It was just—"
- Simera: "Don't lie to me. Not now. How long?"
- Liz: (breaking) "Since... I don't know. A while."
- Simera: "Since when exactly?"
- Liz: "Does it matter?"
- Simera: "Yes. It matters."
- Liz: "A few days after we started going to meetings."
- Simera: (devastated) "You never stopped."
- Liz: "I tried to stop. I wanted to stop."
- Simera: "But you didn't."
- Liz: "No. I didn't."

**The content**:

**The nihilism**:

- Liz: "The Mementos let me not-exist while still existing."
- Liz: "That's all I want. To not be here but also not be dead."

- No solution
- Just continuing in crisis
