## Chapter Summary
**MAJOR REVISION**: This chapter is no longer about Simera and Liz's personal drama. **Ed makes national news—he recorded a Memento of himself assassinating the president.** The event spreads extremely fast, companies unable to prevent it. It becomes the most viewed Memento ever, demonstrating that more shock = better memento economics. **Ed is killed by police immediately after.** The chapter plays half for laughs, half for drama—absurd and tragic simultaneously. Simera and Liz watch it unfold on news, process what Ed did. The memento spreads despite attempts to suppress it. The creator community explodes with reactions. This shows the extreme end of creator economy: someone monetized political assassination. **Ed's final act proves the system's darkest truth: extremity sells, consequences don't matter if you're dead, impact is the only currency.** We don't hear about Ed after this chapter.
