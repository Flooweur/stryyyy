CHAPTER 10: THE DISAPPEARING
=============================

CHAPTER PITCH:
Over the next month, alone in her apartment with broken ribs healing slowly, Simera descends into a fugue state of consuming Mementos constantly—illegal deaths, murders, falls, overdoses—spending her 43,000 units in a blur of borrowed experiences until she can no longer distinguish her memories from purchased ones, often waking with the neural connector attached and hours gone, reality fracturing into fragments where she doesn't recognize her apartment, doesn't remember if she killed someone or watched someone die, doesn't know if the bruises are from her falls or someone else's violence, and in one horrifying moment she looks in the mirror and doesn't recognize her own face, seeing instead the faces of all the people whose deaths she's consumed, until even her name—Simera—sounds like something invented, something that belongs to someone who no longer exists.

EXHAUSTIVE INSTRUCTIONS & IDEAS:

STRUCTURE:

FINAL NOTE:
This chapter is the horror of self-dissolution. Simera has consumed so many deaths that she's died herself, just hasn't stopped breathing yet. The reader should feel her disorientation, uncertainty, fragmentation. End with her almost ready for the Finale, the "not yet" barely holding. The next chapter will push her past that threshold into "yes."
